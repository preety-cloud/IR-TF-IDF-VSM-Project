Weeding the Collection:  A Review of Research on Identifying Obsolete Stock .A Seymour, C.A. Reverberations of the publishing explosion of recent years are reaching us now.  Production of monographs and journals, research reports and government documents has been increasing exponentially to the point that British university librarians are beginning to feel the sense of constriction of space which their American counterparts have been struggling with for a number of years (judging by the amount of print devoted to the subject in American library journals).  The force of this sense of constriction has been somewhat mitigated in all but the copyright libraries by the general lack of funds available for book purchasing, and by the increasing price of books.
