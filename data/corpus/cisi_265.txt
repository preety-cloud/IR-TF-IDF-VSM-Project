Code of Cataloging Rules .A Lubetzky, S. The following rules represent a system designed to produce an instrument essential to the operations and services of a library -- its catalog.  To understand the rules and to apply them properly, it is necessary to comprehend the objectives which the catalog is to serve, the method by which these objectives are to be achieved, the basic aspects of the problem of cataloging, and the general principles which underlie the rules. Objectives.  The objectives which the catalog is to serve are two: First, to facilitate the location of a particular publication, i.e. of a particular edition of a work, which is in the library.
