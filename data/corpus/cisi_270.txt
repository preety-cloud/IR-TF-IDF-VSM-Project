Conflict in Libraries .A Bundy, Mary Lee Intergroup conflict in libraries is explored, including conflict between departments, between professionals and bureaucracy, and between older and newer staff members.. Other special interests such as informal power-holders and the subprofessional are identified.. This analysis shows that existing organizational relationships in libraries let "means" become "ends".. Strong forces toward conformity hamper desirable growth and change.. A restructuring of libraries is proposed along the lines of professional rather than semiprofessional organizations..
