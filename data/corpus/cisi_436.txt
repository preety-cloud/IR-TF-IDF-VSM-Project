Industrial Research and Technological Innovation an Econometric Analysis .A Mansfield, E. After many years of neglect, technological change is receiving the attention from economists that it deserves, the 1960's being a time of a enormous interest in this area in academic, government, and business circles.. Central to the economics of technological change is the manner in which new processes and products are conceived, developed, commercialized, and accepted.. To help promote a better understanding of this process, I have been engaged for a number of years in a series of related econometric studies of industrial research and technological innovation..
