Information Retrieval Systems .A Lancaster, F.W. This book is concerned primarily with those "intellectual" factors that significantly affect the performance of all information retrieval systems; namely,  - indexing policy and practice - vocabulary control - searching strategies - interaction between the system and its users  My viewpoint is that of the evaluator of information systems.  I have therefore paid considerable attention to a discussion of the requirements of users of information systems and the measurement of system performance in terms of the efficient and economical satisfaction of these requirements. The book does not concern itself, except indirectly, with equipment for the implementation of retrieval systems, a topic that is adequately covered by other volumes in this seris.
