Optimum Procedures for Economic Information Retrieval .A Heaps, H. S. Thiel, L. H. Computer searches on Chemical Titles tapes for current awareness at the University of Alberta since May, 1968, have provided sufficient data to allow formulation of a cost function based on the number of titles searched, the length of questions, the extent to which questions may be batched, and certain details regarding the allowed forms of question.. With different choice of parameters the cost function could be adapted to apply to searches of other data bases.. An attempt to make retrospective searching economically feasible has led to formulation of different search techniques.. It is suggested that the search procedure should be designed to minimize computation time at the expense of convenience in the form of output, but that facilities should be included so that the user who is willing to play the additional cost may receive output in a more convenient form..
