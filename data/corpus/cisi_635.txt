Clustering of Scientific Journals .A Carpenter, Mark P. Narin, Francis A cluster analysis procedure is described in which 188 journals in the discipline of physics, chemistry and molecular biology are grouped into clusters.. Most of the clusters are easily identified as subdisciplinary subject areas.. The data source was the cross citing amongst the journals derived from the Journal Citation Index (JCI), a file derived in turn from the Science Citation Index (SCI).. The JCI consists of journal by journal tabulation of citings to and from each journal processed in the SCI.. Two-step citation maps linking the clusters are presented for each discipline..
