Documentation Notes .A Cawkell, A.E. I would suggest three possible reasons for the constant average number of references per article.  Firstly, on average, articles more than fifteen years old rarely get cited as they are rendered obsolete by the rate of advance.  The net increase per annum of 'citeable articles' is not as large as might otherwise be expected. Secondly many relatively small specialized subject areas, each tending to have its own literature and 'internal' citation practices, are hiving off from the classical areas, because of the specialized nature of modern science.
