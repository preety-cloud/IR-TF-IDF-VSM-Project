Language and Mind .A Chomsky, N. The three chapters of this book are somewhat elaborated versions of three lectures, the Beckman lectures, that I delivered at the University of California, at Berkeley, in January 1967.  The first is an attempt to evaluate past contributions to the study of mind that have been been based on research and speculation regarding the nature of language.  The second is devoted to contemporary developments in linguistics that have a bearing on the study of the mind.  The third is a highly speculative discussion of directions that the study of language and mind might take in coming years.
