Library Objectiveness and Performance Measures and Their Use in Decision Making .A Hamburg, Morris .A Ramist, Leonard E. .A Bommer, Michael R. W. For optimal allocations of limited funds, it is necessary for libraries to develop measures of output.. Various forms of user exposure to documents are discussed in an effort to develop such measures for public libraries.. It is suggested that the actual method of accounting  be used to compare such measures with costs, and an illustrative computation is presented.. It is shown how size of user population, amount of exposure, and costs for a given year can be estimated..Similar techniques are suggested for evaluation of library programs.. This approach is then compared with current concepts of library standards..
